The Origin:

Long before the burning of Iron on animal skin for tagging ended, it is usual to see "A-46" marks on livestock in Aleosan. Before Aleosan became known as a place for tourism, Aleosanon were an agricultural melting pot— and Aleosanon are proud that they continue to be one. UK Peak, before it became a tourist attraction, was a traverse route for farmers and livestock buyers from different areas in the Province and Mindanao— now to the whole country. Aleosan may not be a place where you can see haciendas or big livestock farms. Still, it is home to hundreds of these animals owned not by "haciendas" but those we consider "small-time farmers"— whose main source of income and livelihood depends on, and whose lives changed for the better. Farmers who may not own hundreds of cows, but those who built a different kind of bond with the one he has— sometimes end up naming them. A46 is not just a typical restaurant; it celebrate the end of animal cruelty, along with the continuous development of Aleosan's Tourism and Agriculture sectors; and be a testament of Aleosan's humble beginnings.
